🗺️ Your Step-by-Step Route
1
🚉
Start at Parabiago Station
Head to Parabiago train station. You'll be taking the Passante Ferroviario. Look for platforms serving the 🚆 S5 line toward Treviglio / Pioltello (direction: Milan city centre).

Trains run frequently — check the timetable board on the platform.

Board the 🚆 S5 direction Treviglio / Milan. The ride takes approximately 40–50 minutes. You'll pass through several stops — do not get off until Repubblica.

3
🟡
Take Metro M3 → Rogoredo
Exit the train and follow signs for Metro M3 🟡 M3 Yellow Line.

Take M3 in the direction of San Donato and ride it to the last stop: Rogoredo FS. The ride is about 10 minutes and 6 stops.

💡 Tip: buy a single ticket (€2.20) at the machine — it covers both the metro and the whole urban network. If you're a group, a carnet of 10 tickets is more economical.
📍 Rogoredo Station on Maps 🗺️ Directions Repubblica→Rogoredo
4
🚌
Shuttle Bus from Rogoredo
Once at Rogoredo FS, look for the event shuttle (navetta) 🚌 Navetta to Pala Santa Giulia.

The shuttle stop is right outside the station exit — just follow the crowd or look for signage with the hockey or arena logo. Shuttles run frequently before and after the game. It's a short ride (~5 min).

💡 Can't find the shuttle? The arena is also reachable on foot from Rogoredo in about 15–20 minutes following Via Rogoredo → Via Mattei.

💡 Handy Tips for Sunday
  • Buy your ATM Milano metro ticket (€2.20) at any station machine — it covers buses and metro for 90 min.
  • The S5 Passante trains run every 15–30 min on Sunday — check Trenord app for the live timetable.
  • Leave Parabiago at least 2 hours before game time to arrive comfortably and find your seats.
  • At Repubblica, look for yellow M3 signs. The metro connection is inside the same station.
  • Keep your ticket — inspectors are active on Milan transit 🎫
  • For the return trip, shuttles back to Rogoredo run after the final whistle.
  • No Wi-Fi? Screenshot this page or save it offline before you go!
